The Problem

When the Proof Behind a Claim Can't Be Found

Every label makes promises: tested, certified, organic, sustainable, clean. Buildings, hotels, and products carry marks that make the same promise on their behalf. Consumers, retailers, auditors, and regulators increasingly go looking for the evidence, and what they find today is a dead end: no results published, a logo with no way to confirm it's still valid, a PDF from two years ago, or a lookup demanding a lot code and a login.

That gap used to cost trust. Now it costs market access. EU regulation is tightening and US disclosure laws are multiplying, category by category, and the public, current, ungated proof page is becoming a legal requirement. Enforcement is no longer theoretical: warning letters have gone out, brands have been graded publicly on the quality of their disclosure, and the first sales bans arrive in 2027.

When questions surface publicly, whoever defends the claim needs a verifiable, timestamped artifact. IntegraLayer exists so that artifact is already live before anyone asks.

Regulatory Context

Verification Is Becoming the Baseline.

Regulation is converging on one principle: a claim has to be independently verifiable. In the US, that principle is now statute. California's AB 899 already requires baby food brands to publish per-lot heavy-metal results linked from a code on the label, five more states have enacted the same template, and enforcement is active. From January 1, 2027, California SB 646 extends it to prenatal vitamins: per-lot results on an ungated public page, a web address printed on the package, and a sales ban for non-compliance. A pending bill would carry the template to protein products next. In the EU, the Empowering Consumers Directive applies from September 2026: sustainability labels are only permitted when backed by a third-party certification scheme with publicly available terms, which puts every certification mark, from product labels to building and hospitality standards, under the same expectation of public proof.

The bar these laws set for verification infrastructure is specific: public, machine-readable, current, accessible without a gate, and persistent. Self-hosted PDFs, login-walled portals, and lot-code lookups clear at most one or two of those. IntegraLayer is built to clear all of them.

That is the bar for a verifiable claim, not a feature request.

How It Works

Four steps. Zero ambiguity.

01

Input

The source of truth creates the record, exactly as it does today: your lab issues a COA, your certifier issues a certificate, your program updates a status. It reaches IntegraLayer directly from you, your manufacturer, or the issuer. No migration, no new workflow.

02

Process

We parse the record, format it to whatever statute or standard applies, and stage the page for your approval. Every entry is timestamped and tamper-evident. No login, no lot code, no permission needed to verify.

03

Output

A verification page under your name, on your domain: current status, per-lot results where relevant, the issuer behind it, and the date. A QR code points at it, whether newly printed or already in the wild. Where a law requires an on-pack link, the page satisfies it.

04

Control

You control everything. Issuers issue, update, expire, and revoke. Records delivered issuer-direct carry the highest verification tier, because no one has touched them. IntegraLayer only reflects what the source reports. We never interpret, score, or override.
