Who We Are

Neutral Infrastructure for Certification Verification

Designed for certification authorities.

IntegraLayer is a neutral infrastructure for public verification of certification status. It enables certification bodies to make certificate status independently verifiable through a public reference page. Authority remains with the certifier. IntegraLayer does not issue certifications and does not judge compliance.

The Problem

When Certification Claims Can't Be Verified Publicly

Certification marks are relied on by consumers, auditors, and regulators to confirm valid participation in a certification program. When certificates expire or are revoked, marks often remain visible — with no public way to confirm current status.

Verification falls back to internal lookups, emails, or static PDFs. There is no neutral reference point. When questions surface publicly, Legal is left to defend mark usage without a verifiable artifact. That's when it reaches the Board.

How It Works

Four steps. Zero ambiguity.

01

Input

The certification body provides certificate data — holder name, certification type, issue date, expiration, and current status.

02

Process

Certificate data is cryptographically hashed and time-stamped. A unique QR code is generated and linked to a public verification endpoint. The hash is written to an immutable blockchain record.

03

Output

A certifier-branded public verification page showing current status — valid, expired, or revoked — accessible to anyone who scans the QR code.

04

Control

The issuing organization controls everything. They issue, update, expire, and revoke. IntegraLayer only reflects what the certifier reports. We never interpret, score, or override.
